A Network Software Developers

Tunaweza is a Network where Software Developers from all over the world can share ideas, learn new technologies, contribute articles and discuss technology trends. We will attract users by providing free quality training in Java and related technologies and target usiversity students and recent graduates. We will also make our training available to anybody with an aptitude for development of software. We are confident that we can build a user base of 50,000+ users from ten different countries within 24 months. As confirmation of this, Tunaweza went live in Kenya February 2014 and by the end of April (8 weeks) we had 1,234 registered users.

Click here to download the plan

THE OPPORTUNITY.

Tunaweza is a specialist network which provides forums and training in different technologies centered on Java. Users from different countries can attend courses, post ideas and comments, obtain advice and publish apps. This will provide a valuable source of information for marketing and social analysis in this niche area and give an opportunity to influence the opinion of this important group worldwide.

WE DELIVER.

Tunaweza Training Methodology was developed from JJTEACH, a proven system developed by JJPeople which has successfully trained thousands of Java Developers in the UK, Canada and in Africa since 2003. Our hands-on approach to training together with the Tunaweza Advanced Student Management, Private Tuition, Assessments and Experience Engineering systems will form the basis of of our ongoing success.

OUR USERS

Tunaweza targets 3rd and 4th year university computer science students and recent graduates and gives them the opportunity to work on collaborative projects in the real world. With our propriety LMS, we teach technologies and methods not normally found in the university curriculum. In this way Tunaweza is building a network of software developers who will make up a user-base, which – in the years to come - will become senior and significant influencers in this important industry secto

SHORT HISTORY

A short history of the Tunaweza project to date and the present status of the registered users

  • 18th January 2014: The Tunaweza team completed the re-write of the Tunaweza LMS system in January 2014 and the system went live in beta-test. Two hundred invitations were sent to ex-trainees of the JJTeach system telling them of our plans for Tunaweza and enclosing a unique 'use once only' invitation to pass onto a friend or family members.
  • 14th February 2014: We had 52 registered beta-testers giving over 25% positive response and sent another 100 invitation letters to ex-employees.
  • 1st March 2014: We had 92 registered beta-testers.
  • 28th March 2014: We had 102 registered beta-testers and were confident enough on the performance of the software and the support teams to declare the betatest period complete and we rolled out Tunaweza version 2.0 which removed the restriction on registrations.
  • 18th April 2014: We posted advertisements on 26 universities in Kenya and placed a quarter-page advert in the Nation Newspaper (which has the widest circulation of daily papers in Kenya).
  • 2nd May 2014: We achieved 1,234 registered users.
